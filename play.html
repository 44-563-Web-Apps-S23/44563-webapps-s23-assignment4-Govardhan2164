THE BOOR
_Anton Tchekov_

TIME: _The present._

SCENE: _A well-furnished reception-room in_ MRS. POPOV'S _home_.

LUKA

It isn't right, ma'am. You're wearing yourself out!

MRS.POPOV

And I shall never leave it--why should I? My life is over.

LUKA

There you are again! It's too awful to listen to, so it is! Nikolai Michailovitch is dead;

MRS.POPOV

[_Resolutely._]

Please don't speak of these things again.

LUKA

What is the use of all these words, when you'd so much rather go walking in the garden or order Tobby or Welikan harnessed to the trap, and visit the neighbors?

MRS.POPOV

[_Weeping._] Oh!

LUKA

Madam, dear madam, what is it? In Heaven's name!

MRS.POPOV

He loved Tobby so! He always drove him to the Kortschagins or the Vlassovs.

LUKA

Yes, ma'am.

[_A bell rings loudly._

MRS.POPOV

[_Shudders._]

What's that? I am at home to no one.

LUKA

Yes, ma'am.

[_He goes out, centre._

MRS.POPOV

[_Gazing at the photograph._]

You shall see, Nikolai, how I can love and forgive!

[LUKA _enters in great excitement_.
